Discover Yala



Welcome to Yala National Park, Sri Lanka's most visited wildlife sanctuary and home to one of the world's highest densities of leopards. This vast protected area combines sweeping grasslands, lagoons, and dense forest, creating diverse habitats for an incredible variety of wildlife.


Experience the wilderness with our expert safari guides:


  • Early morning and evening game drives for optimal wildlife viewing
  • Tracking of leopards and other big game with experienced rangers
  • Bird watching at coastal wetlands and lagoons
  • Photography guidance at prime wildlife spots

Must-Visit Destinations

Explore the diverse ecosystems and wildlife of Yala

Yala Safari Experience

Embark on an exciting 4x4 safari through Sri Lanka's most visited national park. Known for having the world's highest density of leopards, Yala also offers sightings of elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and numerous bird species. Best experienced during early morning or late afternoon for optimal wildlife viewing.

Situlpawwa Ancient Monastery

An ancient rock temple monastery dating back to the 2nd century BC. Located within Block 1, this archaeological site offers a unique blend of cultural heritage and wildlife. The elevated position provides panoramic views of the surrounding wilderness. Watch for resident troops of monkeys and numerous bird species.

Coastal Habitats & Beaches

Explore Yala's pristine coastline where dense jungle meets the Indian Ocean. These areas are important nesting sites for sea turtles and home to saltwater crocodiles. The rocky outcrops and beaches provide excellent opportunities to spot shore birds and marine life. Spectacular sunset views make this area perfect for photography.

Patanangala Viewpoint

A dramatic coastal viewpoint offering panoramic views of the Indian Ocean and surrounding landscape. This elevated position is excellent for spotting marine life and coastal birds. The area is also known for frequent leopard sightings, as these big cats often patrol the rocky outcrops during early morning and late evening.
