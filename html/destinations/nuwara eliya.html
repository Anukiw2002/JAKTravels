Discover Nuwara Eliya



Welcome to Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka's charming hill station nestled 6,128 feet above sea level. Known as 'Little England' for its colonial architecture and cool climate, this picturesque town offers a unique blend of British heritage, stunning tea plantations, and natural beauty.


Experience the highlands with our guided tours:


  • Tea factory visits and tasting sessions
  • Scenic walks through manicured gardens
  • Colonial architecture exploration
  • Mountain hiking and nature trails
  • Golf at Asia's oldest highland course

Must-Visit Destinations

Explore the finest attractions of Sri Lanka's hill country

Ramboda Falls

One of the highest waterfalls in Sri Lanka at 109 meters, Ramboda Falls cascades in three stages. Located near the Ramboda Pass, visitors can view the falls from different vantage points. The nearby tea factory and hotel offer excellent viewing locations while enjoying refreshments.

Pedro Tea Estate & Factory

Experience one of Sri Lanka's finest tea estates where you can tour a working tea factory, learn about the tea-making process, and taste freshly made Ceylon tea. The estate offers breathtaking views of rolling tea plantations and misty mountains. Morning visits offer the best chance to see the tea plucking in action.

Victoria Park

This beautifully maintained colonial-era park is famous for its rare bird species and exotic flowers. During spring (March to May), the park comes alive with colorful blooms. It's also one of the best places in Sri Lanka to spot hill country birds like the Kashmir Flycatcher and Indian Blue Robin.

Gregory Lake

A picturesque man-made lake offering boat rides, water sports, and scenic walking paths. The lake area features beautiful gardens, an open-air food court, and excellent photo opportunities. Visit during sunset for the most spectacular views and pleasant temperatures for lakeside walks.

Devon Falls

This stunning 97-meter waterfall, named after a pioneer coffee planter, offers spectacular views from the dedicated Devon Falls View Point along the main road. Best viewed while enjoying a cup of tea at the nearby Mlesna Tea Castle. Most impressive during the monsoon season from June to September.

Horton Plains National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site featuring World's End, a dramatic 4,000-foot cliff drop. The 9km hiking trail takes you through cloud forests to Baker's Falls and offers incredible views. Start early (before 6 AM) to avoid the mist and see the best views.

Hakgala Botanical Gardens

One of the highest botanical gardens in the world, featuring roses, orchids, and rare mountain species. These terraced gardens offer stunning views and are particularly beautiful during the spring blooming season. The misty mountain backdrop makes for excellent photography.

St. Clair's Falls

Known as the 'Little Niagara of Sri Lanka', this majestic 80-meter waterfall consists of two cascades nicknamed 'the Grand' and 'the Small'. Easily visible from the Hatton-Talawakele road, it offers excellent photo opportunities. The falls are particularly impressive during the rainy season.
