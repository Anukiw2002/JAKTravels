Discover Mirissa



Welcome to Mirissa, a tropical paradise on Sri Lanka's south coast, renowned for its whale watching opportunities and pristine beaches. This coastal haven offers the perfect blend of marine adventures and relaxed beach life.


Essential experiences in Mirissa:


  • World-famous whale and dolphin watching
  • Swimming and surfing at the main beach
  • Spectacular sunset viewpoints
  • Fresh seafood dining by the beach

Must-Visit Destinations

Essential experiences in Mirissa's coastal paradise

Whale Watching

Mirissa's premier attraction and one of the world's best locations for watching blue whales and sperm whales. The season runs from November to April, with peak sightings from December to March. Early morning tours (starting at 6:30 AM) offer the best conditions and highest chances of sightings. Besides whales, you're likely to spot dolphins, flying fish, and various sea birds.

Mirissa Main Beach

The heart of Mirissa's beach life, this stunning crescent-shaped beach offers something for everyone. The calm eastern end is perfect for swimming and sunbathing, while the western end provides excellent surfing opportunities from November to April. The beach comes alive at sunset with beachfront restaurants serving fresh seafood and tropical cocktails. The perfect spot to experience Sri Lanka's legendary sunsets.

Coconut Tree Hill

The most iconic viewpoint in Mirissa and one of Sri Lanka's most photographed locations. This palm tree-covered promontory offers breathtaking ocean views and is especially magical during sunrise. Visit early morning (before 8 AM) to avoid crowds and catch the best light for photos. During whale watching season, you might even spot whales breaching in the distance.

Parrot Rock

This distinctive rock formation, accessible via a sandy causeway from the main beach, offers spectacular 360-degree views of the coastline. Best visited during low tide, the rock features a viewing platform perfect for sunset watching and photography. Time your visit according to tide tables, and enjoy views of both the main beach and the endless Indian Ocean.
