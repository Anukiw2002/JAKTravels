The Cultural Heart of Sri Lanka

Discover Kandy



Welcome to Kandy, the last royal capital of Sri Lanka, nestled among misty hills at 500 meters above sea level. This UNESCO World Heritage site is the custodian of some of Buddhism's most sacred relics and vibrant Kandyan cultural traditions.


Experience the cultural capital with our curated highlights:


  • Sacred Buddhist temples and ceremonies
  • Traditional Kandyan dance performances
  • Royal Botanical Gardens exploration
  • Cultural heritage walks
  • Scenic lake views and walking paths

Must-Visit Destinations

Explore the cultural and historical treasures of the ancient kingdom

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

The Sri Dalada Maligawa, housing Buddha's sacred tooth relic, is Sri Lanka's most important Buddhist shrine. Visit during the morning or evening puja (offering) ceremonies to experience the rituals. The temple complex showcases some of the finest traditional Kandyan architecture and contains several museums and shrines.

Royal Botanical Gardens, Peradeniya

Once reserved for Kandyan royalty, these 147-acre gardens are among Asia's finest. Famous for their orchid collection, giant Javan fig tree, and avenue of royal palms. The gardens contain over 4,000 species of plants, including rare and endemic tropical species. Best visited in the morning to avoid afternoon heat.

Kandy Lake

Created in 1807 by the last Kandyan king, this artificial lake is a scenic centerpiece of the city. The 3.4km walking path around the lake offers excellent views of the Temple of the Tooth and city. Best enjoyed at sunset when the atmosphere is cooler and the light is perfect for photography.

Kandyan Cultural Show

Experience traditional Sri Lankan performing arts at the Kandyan Art Association. The one-hour show features various forms of traditional dance, drums, and fire walking. Shows are held daily at 5:30 PM, showcasing cultural dances that were once performed for the Kandyan kings.

Gadaladeniya Temple

Built in 1344, this ancient temple showcases a unique blend of Sinhalese, Dravidian, and Thai architectural styles. The temple features impressive stone carvings and ancient murals. Located 15km from Kandy, it forms part of the temple triangle along with Lankathilaka and Embekke Temples.

Bahirawakanda Temple

Situated on a hill overlooking the city, this temple features a large Buddha statue visible from most of Kandy. The climb offers panoramic views of the city, lake, and surrounding mountains. Best visited early morning or late afternoon for comfortable temperatures and better lighting.
