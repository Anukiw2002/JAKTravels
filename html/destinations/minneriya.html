Discover Minneriya



Welcome to Minneriya National Park, home to one of Asia's most spectacular natural events - The Great Elephant Gathering. During the dry season (July to October), hundreds of elephants migrate here to feed on the grass left behind by the receding water of the ancient Minneriya Tank, creating the largest gathering of Asian elephants in the world.

Must-Visit Attractions

Experience the natural wonders of Minneriya

The Great Elephant Gathering

Witness up to 300 elephants congregating around the ancient Minneriya Tank during the dry season. This spectacular gathering is the largest recurring concentration of Asian elephants in the world. Watch as herds socialize, play, and bathe in the waters. Best viewed during late afternoon when elephants emerge from the forest.

Ancient Minneriya Tank

This massive man-made reservoir, built in the 3rd century AD by King Mahasena, creates a unique ecosystem that sustains countless species. The receding waters during dry season create perfect grazing grounds for elephants and attract numerous water birds. Stunning sunset views across the water make for perfect photography opportunities.

Bird Watching Paradise

Explore the park's rich avian diversity with over 160 species of birds, including endemic species like the Sri Lanka Jungle Fowl and Grey Hornbill. The wetland areas around the tank are particularly rich in water birds, including painted storks, large cormorants, and various species of herons. Early morning safaris offer the best bird watching opportunities.
