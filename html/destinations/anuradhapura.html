Discover Anuradhapura



Step into Anuradhapura, where colossal dagobas touch the clouds and ancient monasteries whisper tales of a civilization that rivaled Rome and Egypt. This UNESCO World Heritage site offers an unforgettable journey through Sri Lanka's golden age, where you can walk among architectural marvels that have stood for over two millennia.


Experience the magic with our guided tours:


  • Sunrise photography at ancient monuments
  • Participate in traditional Buddhist ceremonies
  • Explore hidden monastery ruins in the jungle
  • Discover ancient engineering marvels
  • Meet local monks and learn about temple life

Must-Visit Destinations

Discover the most awe-inspiring ancient wonders of Sri Lanka

Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi

Experience the mystical presence of the world's oldest living human-planted tree, grown from the Buddha's original Bodhi tree. Join pilgrims in their sacred rituals and feel the powerful spiritual energy that has drawn believers for over 2,000 years. Best visited at dawn when monks perform their morning ceremonies.

Ruwanweliseya

Marvel at this massive pure white dome that seems to glow in the sunlight. Walk barefoot on ancient stones alongside elephants carved in relief, and join locals as they meditate in the evening. Perfect for sunset photography when the stupa takes on a golden hue.

Mihintale

Climb the 1,840 ancient stone steps to reach the birthplace of Sri Lankan Buddhism. Enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding jungle and ancient city. Time your visit for sunrise to experience an unforgettable morning spectacle.

Abhayagiri Complex & Museum

Explore this vast archaeological wonderland with its famous moonstone carvings, meditation walkways, and ancient monastic buildings. Visit the museum to see priceless artifacts and learn about life in ancient Sri Lanka. Don't miss the incredible stone carvings and guard stones.

Twin Ponds (Kuttam Pokuna)

Witness the engineering genius of ancient Sri Lanka at these stunning twin bathing pools. Marvel at the perfectly preserved stone carvings and sophisticated water filtration system that still works after 1,200 years. Best photographed in early morning light.

Isurumuniya Rock Temple

Discover this unique temple carved into natural rock, famous for its exquisite stone carvings including the celebrated 'Isurumuniya Lovers'. Set beside a lotus pond with resident monkeys, it's particularly beautiful during sunset when the rocks glow orange.
